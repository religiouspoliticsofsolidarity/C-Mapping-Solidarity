"The march was precisely to remember the victims of the fire in Ciudad Juárez, the 40 men and the women who also listened and saw all the suffering and the families, to remember that this happened under the Biden administration, because precisely the United States was pushing Mexico to detain more people at the borders so they would not cross, and it was precisely also to denounce the criminalization of organizations such as Casa Anunciación and other organizations that were doing the work of faith, the work that Catholics would call works of mercy, and that for this reason were being criminalized".

“We had the gathering between religious and some secular people and asked for the opportunity to enter one of the detention centers where they had detained exclusively women and children, or mothers with their children. I approached a Central American woman and asked her, what can I do for you? And she said to me, ‘Remember this number, Father, remember it, remember it.’ She continued talking and I kept thinking I must not forget the number. I memorized it and contacted the family.".

"Because we were not going to save the people, we were not going to empower them, on the contrary, we were going to walk together, we were going to understand what was happening, how we could collaborate, how we could open doors for one another, with my companions in each of these countries, for example, since they have been the driving force of the action. We have only helped to implement, right? For example, when it was necessary to open doors in the United States".
